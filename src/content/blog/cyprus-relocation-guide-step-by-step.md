Relocating to a new country is exciting but overwhelming. Cyprus makes it relatively straightforward, but there are specific steps you need to follow. This guide walks you through the entire process.

## Step 1: Understand Your Visa Options

Your visa pathway depends on your nationality and purpose of stay.

- EU/EEA citizens: Free to live and work. Register with the Civil Registry after 3 months.
- Non-EU citizens: Several options available — employment visa, self-employment permit, digital nomad visa, or permanent residency through investment.
- Digital Nomad Visa: Available for remote workers. Valid for 1 year, renewable for 2 more.

## Step 2: Find Your Home

Start your property search before arriving if possible. Consider:

- Rent first, buy later — get to know the areas before committing
- Factor in commute times, school proximity, and lifestyle preferences
- Budget 1 month's rent as a deposit plus 1 month in advance
- Average rental for a 2-bed in Limassol: €800–€1,400/month

## Step 3: Get Your Tax Identification Number (TIN)

Once you arrive, visit the Tax Department to apply for your TIN. You'll need:

- Passport or EU ID
- Proof of address (rental agreement)
- Employment contract or business registration

This is essential for opening a bank account and accessing services.

## Step 4: Register for Social Insurance

Visit the Social Insurance office to register. If you're employed, your employer handles contributions. Self-employed individuals must register independently.

## Step 5: Open a Bank Account

Major banks in Cyprus include Bank of Cyprus, Hellenic Bank, and Eurobank. You'll need:

- Passport and proof of address
- TIN number
- Employment contract or proof of income
- Reference letter from your previous bank (some banks require this)

The process can take 1-3 weeks due to compliance checks.

## Step 6: Set Up Utilities

- Electricity: Register with EAC (Electricity Authority of Cyprus)
- Water: Register with your local water board
- Internet: Major providers include Cyta, Epic, and PrimeTel

## Step 7: Register Your Children in School

Cyprus has excellent international schools. Popular options in Limassol include:

- Heritage Private School
- Foley's Grammar School
- The Grammar School Limassol
- Silverline Private School
- Pascal English School

Application deadlines vary, so start early. Public schools are free for residents.

## Step 8: Get Health Insurance

EU citizens can use the public healthcare system (GESY). Non-EU residents should arrange private health insurance. Annual premiums start from around €500 per person.
