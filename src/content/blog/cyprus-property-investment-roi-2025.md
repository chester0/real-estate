Cyprus continues to attract property investors from across Europe, the Middle East, and beyond. But where should you invest in 2025? Let's break down the numbers city by city.

## Why Cyprus for Property Investment?

Before diving into specifics, here's why Cyprus stands out:

- EU member state with strong rule of law
- 12.5% corporate tax rate — one of the lowest in Europe
- 60+ double tax treaties
- No inheritance tax
- Growing demand from international professionals and companies
- Tourism sector driving short-term rental demand

## City-by-City Analysis

### Limassol — Stability and Premium Returns

Limassol is the island's most mature property market. Prices are higher, but demand is consistently strong.

- Average price: €3,000–€5,500 per m²
- Long-term rental yield: 4–5.5%
- Capital appreciation: 5–8% annually
- Tenant profile: corporate professionals, expats, international families

Best for: long-term investors seeking stable returns with low vacancy risk.

### Nicosia — Highest Rental Yields

The capital offers the best rental yields in Cyprus, driven by strong local demand and lower purchase prices.

- Average price: €1,500–€2,800 per m²
- Long-term rental yield: 5.5–7%
- Capital appreciation: 3–5% annually
- Tenant profile: government workers, university students, local professionals

Best for: yield-focused investors with a buy-to-let strategy.

### Paphos — Holiday Rental Market

Paphos has a strong tourism base, especially from the UK market. The holiday rental market is well-developed.

- Average price: €1,800–€3,200 per m²
- Short-term rental yield: 6–9% (seasonal)
- Capital appreciation: 3–6% annually
- Tenant profile: tourists, retirees, holiday home buyers

Best for: Airbnb and holiday rental investors comfortable with seasonal fluctuations.

### Larnaca — Emerging Opportunity

Larnaca is the least expensive major city and is developing rapidly. The new marina project and airport proximity drive growth.

- Average price: €1,200–€2,500 per m²
- Long-term rental yield: 5–6.5%
- Capital appreciation: 4–7% annually
- Tenant profile: airport workers, growing expat community, budget-conscious families

Best for: value investors seeking early entry into an appreciating market.

### Ayia Napa — Short-Term ROI Champion

Ayia Napa delivers the highest short-term rental returns but with significant seasonal variation.

- Average price: €2,000–€3,500 per m²
- Short-term rental yield: 8–12% (peak season)
- Capital appreciation: 4–6% annually
- Tenant profile: tourists (April–October)

Best for: investors focused on maximum short-term yield, comfortable with off-season vacancy.

## Key Metrics to Track

When evaluating a Cyprus property investment, focus on:

- Net rental yield (after management fees, maintenance, taxes)
- Occupancy rate (especially for short-term rentals)
- Capital growth trajectory for the area
- Total transaction costs (stamp duty, transfer fees, legal fees — typically 8–12% of purchase price)
- Exit strategy — resale market liquidity in the area
